The Wigner‚ÄìEckart theorem

The Wigner‚ÄìEckart theorem states that the matrix element of a tensor component $\tensor{T}^{(k)}_q$ can be evaluated as

\[\begin{equation} \matrixel{n'j'm'}{\tensor{T}^{(k)}_q}{njm}= (-)^{j'-m'} \begin{pmatrix} j'&k&j\\ -m'&q&m \end{pmatrix} \redmatrixel{n'j'}{\tensor{T}^{(k)}}{nj}, \tag{V13.1.2} \end{equation}\]

where the reduced matrix element $\redmatrixel{n'j'}{\tensor{T}^{(k)}}{nj}$ does not depend on $m,m'$.

Spherical tensors

The spherical tensors are related to the spherical harmonics as

\[\tensor{C}^{(k)}_q \defd \sqrt{\frac{4\pi}{2k+1}} Y^k_q. \tag{V5.1.7}\]

The reduced matrix element of the spherical tensor is given by

\[\begin{aligned} \redmatrixel{\ell'}{\tensor{C}^{(k)}}{\ell} &= \angroot{\ell} C_{\ell 0;k,0}^{\ell'0} = (-)^{\ell-k} \angroot{\ell\ell'} \begin{pmatrix} \ell&k&\ell'\\0&0&0 \end{pmatrix}. \end{aligned} \tag{V13.2.107}\]

Dipole operator

The dipole operator is a rank-1 Cartesian tensor that may be expressed using the rank-1 spherical tensor:

\[\hat{\vec{r}} \equiv \begin{bmatrix}\hat{x}\\\hat{y}\\\hat{z}\end{bmatrix} \equiv \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[-\tensor{C}^{(1)}_1 + \tensor{C}^{(1)}_{-1}]\\ \frac{\im}{\sqrt{2}}[\tensor{C}^{(1)}_1 + \tensor{C}^{(1)}_{-1}]\\ \tensor{C}^{(1)}_0 \end{bmatrix}\]
